## Detail

### RD-DATA-001 — Synthetic uncertainty modelling assumptions
**Severity:** medium  
**Subsystem:** synthetic  
**Discovered:** 2026-06-17T05:51:09.749946+00:00  
**Resolved:** No  

The synthetic geometry generator uses simplified perspective projection formulae. Real-world lens distortion, sensor noise, and environmental occlusion are not modelled. Synthetic samples may not faithfully represent edge cases encountered in production.

### RD-DATA-002 — Coverage thresholds require empirical tuning
**Severity:** medium  
**Subsystem:** coverage  
**Discovered:** 2026-06-17T05:51:09.749967+00:00  
**Resolved:** No  

Current coverage completeness thresholds (distance bins, orientation bins, minimum samples-per-cell) are heuristic. They should be validated against downstream model performance data before being treated as ground truth.

### RD-DATA-003 — Outlier detection sensitivity unvalidated
**Severity:** low  
**Subsystem:** validation  
**Discovered:** 2026-06-17T05:51:09.749970+00:00  
**Resolved:** No  

The IQR-based outlier detection in the validation engine uses a fixed multiplier of 1.5. This may be too aggressive for small datasets or too permissive for large ones. Requires domain-expert calibration per fiducial type.

### RD-DATA-004 — Human approval workflow is file-based
**Severity:** low  
**Subsystem:** approval  
**Discovered:** 2026-06-17T05:51:09.749972+00:00  
**Resolved:** No  

The current human approval mechanism writes a JSON approval record to disk. This is sufficient for research use but does not provide cryptographic non-repudiation of reviewer identity. A signed-approval mechanism should be considered for production.
